# Best Practices Guide

Tips and best practices for using AI Investor effectively and safely.

## Investment Best Practices

### 1. Start with a Plan

- **Define Your Goals**: What are you investing for? (retirement, house, education)
- **Set Time Horizon**: Short-term, medium-term, or long-term?
- **Determine Risk Tolerance**: How much risk can you handle?
- **Create a Budget**: Only invest what you can afford to lose

### 2. Diversify Your Portfolio

- **Don't Put All Eggs in One Basket**: Spread investments across different assets
- **Mix Asset Classes**: Stocks, bonds, ETFs, etc.
- **Geographic Diversification**: Consider international markets
- **Sector Diversification**: Don't focus on one industry

### 3. Do Your Research

- **Read Company Reports**: Annual reports, earnings, financials
- **Follow Market News**: Stay informed about market trends
- **Use AI Insights**: Leverage AI predictions as one tool
- **Understand What You're Buying**: Know the companies you invest in

### 4. Use Risk Management Tools

- **Stop Loss Orders**: Protect against large losses
- **Position Sizing**: Don't risk too much on one trade
- **Portfolio Limits**: Set maximum position sizes
- **Regular Reviews**: Monitor and adjust your portfolio

### 5. Think Long-Term

- **Avoid Day Trading**: Unless you're experienced
- **Focus on Fundamentals**: Long-term value over short-term gains
- **Stay Disciplined**: Stick to your investment plan
- **Ignore Market Noise**: Don't react to every market movement

## Trading Best Practices

### Order Types

**Market Orders**:
- Use for: Quick execution, liquid stocks
- Avoid for: Illiquid stocks, volatile markets

**Limit Orders**:
- Use for: Price protection, better execution
- Set limits: Reasonable prices, not too far from market

**Stop Loss Orders**:
- Always use: For protection against large losses
- Set stops: Based on risk tolerance, not arbitrary levels

### Timing

- **Market Hours**: Trade during regular hours for best liquidity
- **Avoid Volatility**: Be cautious during earnings, news events
- **Don't Chase**: Don't buy just because price is rising
- **Be Patient**: Wait for good entry points

### Position Sizing

- **Risk Per Trade**: Never risk more than 1-2% per trade
- **Maximum Position**: Limit single positions to 5-10% of portfolio
- **Cash Reserve**: Keep some cash for opportunities
- **Rebalance**: Regularly rebalance your portfolio

## Security Best Practices

### Account Security

1. **Strong Password**
   - Use unique, complex password
   - Minimum 12 characters
   - Mix of letters, numbers, symbols
   - Don't reuse passwords

2. **Two-Factor Authentication**
   - Always enable 2FA
   - Use authenticator app (not SMS if possible)
   - Keep backup codes safe

3. **Secure Connection**
   - Always use HTTPS
   - Don't use public WiFi for trading
   - Use VPN if on public networks

4. **Regular Monitoring**
   - Review account activity regularly
   - Check for unauthorized access
   - Monitor email for account changes

### Device Security

- **Keep Software Updated**: Update OS and apps regularly
- **Use Antivirus**: Protect against malware
- **Lock Devices**: Use PIN, fingerprint, or face ID
- **Log Out**: Always log out on shared devices

### Portfolio Management

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Emotional Trading

- **Don't Panic Sell**: During market downturns
- **Don't FOMO Buy**: Chasing hot stocks
- **Stay Disciplined**: Stick to your plan

### 2. Overtrading

- **Avoid Churning**: Don't trade too frequently
- **Let Winners Run**: Don't sell winners too early
- **Cut Losses**: Don't hold losers too long

### 3. Lack of Diversification

- **Don't Concentrate**: Too much in one stock
- **Spread Risk**: Across different assets
- **Rebalance**: Regularly rebalance

### 4. Ignoring Risk

- **Use Stop Losses**: Always protect positions
- **Position Size**: Don't risk too much
- **Understand Risk**: Know what you're risking

### 5. Not Doing Research

- **Don't Blindly Follow**: Tips or recommendations
- **Do Your Own Research**: Understand investments
- **Verify Information**: Check multiple sources
